ABOUT ME

My interest in science started gradually — but it stuck.

Science first, then technology — that’s the order it’s always pulled me in, even if I didn’t realise it right away. I didn’t grow up dreaming of being a scientist (or anything in particular), but by high school, I discovered a love for science — and a knack for it, too.

I studied Biochemistry — my second choice after missing out on Medicine, which turned out to be a blessing. I’m not great with blood, but I took to the lab instantly. From first year, I volunteered in research projects and by graduation, I’d already been cited in a scientific paper.

That momentum led to a PhD in Genetics of Healthy Human Aging at UCL. I spent four fantastic years studying how genes affect longevity using fission yeast, RNA/DNA sequencing, and molecular tools. I earned my doctorate at 25 — a little early, thanks to summer research, scholarships, and consistently being a top student.

Exploring Technology

Over time, my interests expanded beyond biology. Technology started to fascinate me—not as a departure from science, but as a powerful extension of it. From the first black hole image to big data and web development, I was captivated.

Today, I see tech as part of the same journey: helping us live better, longer, more informed lives. That’s what keeps me learning and building.
