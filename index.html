ABOUT ME

My interest in science started gradually, but it’s been a steady source of fascination ever since.

Science first, then technology — that’s the order it’s always pulled me in, even if I didn’t realise it at first. I didn’t grow up dreaming of being a scientist (or anything in particular), but by high school, I discovered a love for science — and a knack for it too.

I studied Biochemistry — my second choice after missing out on Medicine, which turned out to be a blessing. I’ve never been great with blood, but I took to the lab instantly. From my first year, I volunteered in research projects, spent summers in the lab, and by graduation, I’d already been cited in my first paper.

That momentum carried me into a PhD in the Genetics of Healthy Human Aging at UCL. I spent four incredible years studying how genes affect longevity using fission yeast and tools like RNA and DNA sequencing. I earned my doctorate at 25 — a bit ahead of schedule, thanks to summer research, scholarships, and consistently ranking as a top student.

Dr Romila


Welcome to my portal!

Over time, my interests expanded beyond biology. As I moved deeper into research, I found myself increasingly drawn to the world of technology — not as a shift away from science, but as a powerful complement to it.

I became fascinated by how tech can help us ask better questions — and find answers faster. From the first image of a black hole to nanotech in medicine and blockchain’s evolving potential, I’ve been captivated by the pace of innovation. Tools like data analysis, computational biology, and even web development have opened new doors for how we explore and shape human health and longevity.
